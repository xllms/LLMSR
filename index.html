Introduction

LLM-SR aims to generate a controllable and interpretable reasoning process by utilizing structural reasoning. This task can be viewed as a constrained Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning process, where the reasoning unfolds step by step, referencing relevant facts and rules. This approach enhances the transparency and reliability of the reasoning process. Each inference step consists of a “statement,” “evidence,” and “verification,” with the “verification” step determining whether the statement can be logically deduced from the evidence provided.

The first task requires the generation of the “cot_parsing” result based on the content of the "cot." This involves analyzing the multi-step reasoning process. The second task assesses the correctness of the reasoning for each step, evaluating whether the reasoning aligns with the content of the "question," "statement," and "evidence" presented at each stage of the process.

Dataset

For the LLM-SR task, we present a fine-grained Chain-of-Thought (CoT) analysis dataset derived from Llama3, consisting of 24 annotated examples that serve as the training set. These examples are accompanied by reasoning steps, which are integral to the task.
